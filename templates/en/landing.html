European Context

European education systems are operating in a context of accelerated digital transformation, intensified by the COVID-19 crisis and the rapid emergence of generative AI.

  • Persistent gaps in basic skills acquisition
  • Especially among learners from disadvantaged backgrounds
  • Risk of amplifying inequalities without clear pedagogical frameworks
  • Teachers with AI exposure but limited preparedness
Main Objective

Develop and validate an evidence-based, ethical and inclusive framework

For the use of AI-powered personalised learning pathways to support basic skills acquisition in secondary education and initial VET (EQF 2-4), without exacerbating existing inequalities and while reinforcing (not replacing) teachers' pedagogical role.

Key insight: Effective use of PLPs depends less on technological sophistication than on pedagogical integration, teacher agency, ethical governance and contextual adaptation.

Platform Modules

Initial Diagnostic

Questionnaires and diagnostic tests to identify needs, map strengths and weaknesses, and initiate the PLP.

Learning Activities

Brief exercises, micro-tasks, guided reflections and digital challenges during the pathway.

Formative Assessment

Continuous progress tracking to observe evolution and identify persistent difficulties.

Dashboards

Visualization of observable learning signals: responses, attempts, time spent, progression, learning patterns.
